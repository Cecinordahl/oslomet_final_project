Reflection Essay

Learning to Code

It all started with a course at OsloMet called DAPE1400 Programming- a course we were able to follow physically and/or virtually, at our own pace, with a few stepping stones disguised as 'obligs' on the way.

The Process

I had never coded until I started as a first year student at OsloMet. In the beginning of the semester I found coding easier, when we were learning what String, int and double were, the differences between them and how they worked. But as we progressed it became more challenging to understand and keep up. Some subjects took longer to understand than others, like when should a method be static and when should it not.

The Ups and Downs

I feel like learning to code and coding in general is like a road with many highs and lows, ups and downs. When you are stuck and don’t understand right away what is wrong in your code or how things work it can be extremely frustrating. But once you finally figure out what’s the problem and have a working code, it can feel like the biggest accomplishment in the world. There have been many instances throughout the semester where I have been sitting with a problem for way too long without a break. But, when you're sitting there, deeply invested in a piece of the code, it is easy to tell yourself that you will take a break soon. But, soon turns out to be much later. I have realized, those times I have forced myself to take a break, I come back with a much clearer head than before.

Finding a Way

Coding is not necessarily about finding the right way but finding a way. Throughout this semester I have realized there are not just one solution to a coding problem, but many of them. One solution may work better than another, but there are still many ways to go about solving problems. This is where I realized problem solving skills are necessary to become a developer. The more exercises I solved throughout the semester, the better and more comfortable I felt solving more advanced problems. To me, problem solving is all about the process of dissecting a large and complex problem into smaller parts. Solving exercises, re-doing exercises that are not clear the first time and reminding myself that we will never stop learning have been the biggest motivator throughout the semester.

Conclusion

Learning to code can for many be a trial and error process. In the end, we will all find our own way but the best way I found for myself to learn to code was by doing as many exercises and mini-projects as possible. I want to learn more and truly understand what I learn. I want to be good at this. I hope I one day get to work with coding and developing as a full-time job once I graduate from OsloMet.
