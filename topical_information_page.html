Technology and society

Tech is the future

Identifying three universally designed aspects of technology that we encounter in our daily life and explaining how they make our life easier.

The concept behind universal design is easy to understand, but can be hard to implement in a practical way. We have a lot of challenges designing something for everyone, no matter disabilities or challenges of different kinds.(Nesmith,2016) In this text we want to show different forms of universal design and how we get positively affected by this. If you want to read more about making universal design a reality, press here.

A technological example we want elaborate on is auto correct. Autocorrection is a function which is mainly widespread on phones. This allows the telephone to change words as you type and is often activated as a default option. The phones detect when you have written something grammatically wrong and changes it to the word you were most likely supposed to write. This is positive for those that struggles with grammar, but also makes the efficiency faster for those who doesn’t have any problems with it. Do you wanna read more about auto correct? press here.

The second universal design we will consider is audiobooks. Audiobooks isn't just easy to use, but have become very popular lately. They can be downloaded and be used on most digital devices and platforms. If we are to use the terms “temporary handicap” then this is a great design that can be used when you don’t have the ability to read a book. Feel like listening to Audibooks? Try Audible.com. Situations that are perfect while listening, can be while driving a car, on a walk or when we simply must have your attention and eyes on something else.

Third and at last we have chosen the stop-announcing system on public transport. The stopannouncing system is a messaging system which tells us where we are in our route over speakers and on a visual way. If you aren't familiar with the route, have a hearing or a visual disability it helps us follow and know where and when to disembark the vehicle. It also benefits those who know the area with letting them relax and focus on other daily activities.

Universal design can act as a useful solution for an aging population. Think about a friend or family member who is older. List the top 10 ways that universal design can help them live a longer and happier life.

Being old often comes with impairments and disabilities. Old women or men will often struggle with daily tasks that earlier in their life would be considered easy. Some of these can come in form of visual and cognitive impairments or muscles being weakened to mention some. It is therefore important to design a world where we take responsibility and care for the dramatically increasing amount of elders. A product being universal designed, has to be developed such that everyone with or without the same struggles can use them. (National Disability Authority, 2014) We will now list what we think is solutions that is universal and can help the elders, but also aid persons without any form of disability or impairments.

  8. Robot lawnmower and vacuum
  9. Smart homes
